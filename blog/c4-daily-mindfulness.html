Daily Mindfulness

"We have two ears and one mouth so that we can listen twice as much as we speak." -Epictetus

Friday, 8 April 2016

What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'?

It takes a lot energy to have a mindful conversation with someone when the topic isn't of interest to the person. I would often be questioning different things about what my partner was saying. Listening without being distracted by internal chatter is not easy.

How did the other person find it?

My partner was extremely happy to be able to spend 4 minutes monologuing about intercoolers. It was a bit harder for him to loop back to me what I was talking about, so maybe my mindfulness practice is paying off haha.

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

I've been trying to convince my partner to do this meditation thing with me, but so far I haven't had much luck so finding a buddy would probably need to be someone in my cohort. Any takers?

So for now, I'm more inclined to try the 'Do less than you can' and 'Take one breath a day' techniques.

Any other musings?
