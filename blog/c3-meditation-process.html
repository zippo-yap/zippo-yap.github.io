Meditation Process

"Meditation brings wisdom; lack of mediation leaves ignorance. Know well what leads you forward and what hold you back, and choose the path that leads to wisdom." - Buddha

Sunday, 3 April 2016

How did the 'process over product' concept affect the way you tackled the site redesign and rebuild?

The struggle is real. I tried to stick with something simple so that I could "learn the process", but this whole week has been a real challenge. Sometimes even when you think you've got a simple plan, implementing it is so much harder. If level of learning corresponds with the 'struggle' I would say this week has been a success!

What did you think about meditation before reading chapter 2?

To be honest, I've never really thought much of meditation previously. I have friends that are very into their yoga, meditation or chants and it's never been up my alley (even after we did our time and habits sedtion).

What new things have you learnt about meditation?

I really liked the analogy of when you are doing bicep curls, when you extend your arm - you don't consider that failing, and so letting your mind wander and then reeling it back in is actually all part of the meditation process.

Did any of the suggested meditation techniques stand out to you?

I downloaded the Headspace app this week and I've been using it all week except Wednesday and it's actually really helped me! I wasn't very productive on Wednesday, but the rest of the week has worked out really well.
